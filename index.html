About

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Notre Dame in the CoMMaND Lab, where I use computational modeling to study how tissue remodeling affects the mechanical behavior and function of the gastrointestinal tract.

Before this, I completed my PhD at the University of Denver in the Center for Orthopaedic Biomechanics, where I developed patient-specific soft tissue models for surgical planning. I also spent a short time at Microsoft working in product design.

More broadly, I am interested in using computational tools to better understand human physiology and disease, particularly through patient-specific models and mechanics-based approaches that can support diagnosis, treatment, and clinical decision-making.
